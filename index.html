About Me

Introduction

I am a PhD student at the University of Texas Rio Grande Valley (UTRGV) with a passion for research and a deep interest in how intelligent machines can contribute to a better world. My work focuses on learning, cooperation, scalability, and decision-making in multi-agent systems. I am interested in developing algorithms and frameworks that enable autonomous agents to collaborate effectively, adapt to complex environments, and make intelligent decisions that benefit both individuals and the collective system.

Through my research, I aim to advance our understanding of how intelligent systems can cooperate, adapt, and evolve to address real-world challenges. I believe the future of artificial intelligence lies not only in building systems that excel at individual tasks, but also in creating communities of intelligent agents that work together harmoniously to achieve greater shared goals.
